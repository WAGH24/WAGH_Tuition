Chapter 2: Nelson Mandela — Long Walk to Freedom

Chapter NameNelson Mandela: Long Walk to Freedom
Included: Before You Read in-text questions, all Oral Comprehension Checks, and Thinking about the Text. Activities, language work, speaking, writing and poem sections are excluded.

PART I — In-text Questions and Oral Comprehension Checks

✅ Detailed Answer

Apartheid was the officially enforced system of racial segregation in South Africa. It separated people according to race and denied non-white people equal political, social and economic rights.

✅ Final Answer

South Africa had the apartheid system.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: દક્ષિણ આફ્રિકામાં અપાર્થાઇડ પ્રથા હતી.

✅ Detailed Answer

It was the swearing-in ceremony of Nelson Mandela as the first Black President of democratic South Africa on 10 May 1994. It marked the end of white minority rule and the birth of a non-racial democratic government.

✅ Final Answer

It was Nelson Mandela’s inauguration as South Africa’s first Black President.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: આ પ્રસંગ દક્ષિણ આફ્રિકાના પ્રથમ અશ્વેત રાષ્ટ્રપતિ તરીકે નેલ્સન મંડેલાના શપથગ્રહણનો હતો.

✅ Detailed Answer

The ceremonies took place in the sandstone amphitheatre formed by the Union Buildings in Pretoria. In India, examples of sandstone public buildings include the Red Fort, Rashtrapati Bhavan and Parliament House.

✅ Final Answer

The ceremonies took place at the Union Buildings in Pretoria; examples in India include the Red Fort and Rashtrapati Bhavan.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: વિધિ પ્રિટોરિયાની યુનિયન બિલ્ડિંગ્સમાં યોજાઈ હતી; ભારતમાં લાલ કિલ્લો અને રાષ્ટ્રપતિ ભવન તેના ઉદાહરણો છે.

✅ Detailed Answer

South Africa lies in the Southern Hemisphere, where seasons are opposite to those in the Northern Hemisphere. Therefore, May falls in autumn there.

✅ Final Answer

10 May is an autumn day because South Africa is in the Southern Hemisphere.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: દક્ષિણ આફ્રિકા દક્ષિણ ગોળાર્ધમાં હોવાથી 10 મે ત્યાં શરદ ઋતુનો દિવસ છે.

✅ Detailed Answer

The disaster was apartheid, a cruel system of racial oppression. The glorious achievement was the establishment of a democratic, non-racial government based on equality, freedom and human dignity.

✅ Final Answer

The disaster was apartheid; the achievement was the birth of a free, democratic and non-racial South Africa.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: વિપત્તિ અપાર્થાઇડ હતી; સિદ્ધિ સ્વતંત્ર, લોકશાહી અને નિર્વર્ણભેદી દક્ષિણ આફ્રિકાની સ્થાપના હતી.

✅ Detailed Answer

He thanks them for attending the inauguration and sharing in South Africa’s victory over oppression. Their presence showed recognition and support for the new democratic nation.

✅ Final Answer

He thanks them for joining South Africa in celebrating the victory of justice, peace and human dignity.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તેમણે ન્યાય, શાંતિ અને માનવ ગૌરવની જીતની ઉજવણીમાં જોડાવા બદલ આંતરરાષ્ટ્રીય નેતાઓનો આભાર માન્યો.

✅ Detailed Answer

He promises to free all people from poverty, deprivation, suffering, gender discrimination and racial discrimination, and to build a peaceful and democratic nation.

✅ Final Answer

He sets out the ideals of freedom, equality, justice, peace and human dignity for all.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તેમણે સૌ માટે સ્વતંત્રતા, સમાનતા, ન્યાય, શાંતિ અને માનવ ગૌરવના આદર્શો રજૂ કર્યા.

✅ Detailed Answer

The generals salute Mandela and pledge loyalty to the new democratic government. Under apartheid they would have arrested him; their changed attitude reflects the political transformation of the country.

✅ Final Answer

They salute Mandela and pledge loyalty because South Africa now has a democratic government.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તેઓ મંડેલાને સલામ કરે છે અને વફાદારીની પ્રતિજ્ઞા લે છે, કારણ કે હવે દક્ષિણ આફ્રિકામાં લોકશાહી સરકાર છે.

✅ Detailed Answer

The two anthems honoured both communities and symbolised reconciliation. One represented the old Republic and the other the Black freedom struggle, showing unity in the new nation.

✅ Final Answer

The two anthems symbolised reconciliation and unity between Black and white South Africans.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: બે રાષ્ટ્રગીતોએ અશ્વેત અને શ્વેત દક્ષિણ આફ્રિકનો વચ્ચે સમાધાન અને એકતાનું પ્રતીક રજૂ કર્યું.

✅ Detailed Answer

In the first decade, white rulers created a harsh system of racial domination. In the final decade, apartheid was overturned and replaced by a democratic government recognising equal rights for all.

✅ Final Answer

The century began with racial domination and ended with a democratic, non-racial government.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: સદીની શરૂઆત જાતિવાદી શાસનથી થઈ અને અંત લોકશાહી, નિર્વર્ણભેદી સરકારથી થયો.

✅ Detailed Answer

Courage is not the absence of fear but the triumph over it. A brave person may feel afraid, yet conquers that fear to do what is right.

✅ Final Answer

Courage is not absence of fear, but victory over it.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: સાહસ એટલે ભયનો અભાવ નહીં, પરંતુ ભય પર વિજય.

✅ Detailed Answer

Mandela believes love comes more naturally to the human heart. Hatred is learned, so people can also be taught to love.

✅ Final Answer

Mandela believes love is more natural than hate.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: મંડેલા માને છે કે દ્વેષ કરતાં પ્રેમ માનવ હૃદય માટે વધુ સ્વાભાવિક છે.

✅ Detailed Answer

Every person has an obligation to family and another to the people, community and country. Under apartheid, a Black person could not fulfil both freely.

✅ Final Answer

The twin obligations are duty to one’s family and duty to one’s people and country.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: જોડિયા ફરજો એટલે પરિવાર પ્રત્યેની ફરજ અને પોતાના લોકો તથા દેશ પ્રત્યેની ફરજ.

✅ Detailed Answer

As a boy, freedom meant running in fields and living by tribal customs. As a student, it meant temporary personal freedoms. Later he understood basic freedoms as the right to work, marry, support a family and live with dignity.

✅ Final Answer

His idea of freedom grew from temporary personal liberty to basic human dignity and equal rights.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તેમની સ્વતંત્રતાની સમજ તાત્કાલિક વ્યક્તિગત મુક્તિમાંથી મૂળભૂત માનવ ગૌરવ અને સમાન અધિકારો સુધી વિકસતી ગઈ.

✅ Detailed Answer

No. The oppressor becomes a prisoner of hatred, prejudice and narrow-mindedness. By denying another person’s freedom, he loses his own humanity and moral freedom.

✅ Final Answer

No. The oppressor is imprisoned by hatred and prejudice and is therefore not truly free.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: ના. દમનકર્તા દ્વેષ અને પૂર્વગ્રહનો કેદી હોવાથી સાચે સ્વતંત્ર નથી.

PART II — Thinking about the Text

✅ Detailed Answer

They attended to recognise and celebrate South Africa’s peaceful transition from apartheid to democracy. Their presence showed international support for the new nation.

✅ Final Answer

Their attendance signified the triumph of justice, peace, equality and human dignity over apartheid.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તેમની હાજરીએ અપાર્થાઇડ પર ન્યાય, શાંતિ, સમાનતા અને માનવ ગૌરવની જીત દર્શાવી.

✅ Detailed Answer

He means his achievement was not his alone. It rested on the courage, sacrifice and struggle of generations of African patriots, and he saw himself as the continuation of their work.

✅ Final Answer

His success was the result of the sacrifices of countless African freedom fighters before him.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તેમની સફળતા પહેલાંના અસંખ્ય આફ્રિકન સ્વાતંત્ર્યસેનાનીઓના બલિદાનનું પરિણામ હતી.

✅ Detailed Answer

Yes. Severe oppression can bring out courage, endurance and moral strength. Mandela illustrates this through leaders such as Oliver Tambo, Walter Sisulu and Chief Luthuli. Similar examples include Mahatma Gandhi and Martin Luther King Jr.

✅ Final Answer

Mandela shows that great suffering can produce extraordinary courage and character.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: મંડેલા દર્શાવે છે કે મહાન દુઃખ અસાધારણ સાહસ અને ઊંચું ચરિત્ર ઊભું કરી શકે છે.

✅ Detailed Answer

As a child he thought he was naturally free. As a student he wanted personal freedoms. As a young man he realised that the freedom of all Black South Africans had been taken away, so his idea of freedom expanded into a struggle for collective dignity and justice.

✅ Final Answer

His understanding changed from personal freedom to the freedom and dignity of all his people.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તેમની સમજ વ્યક્તિગત સ્વતંત્રતાથી પોતાના તમામ લોકોની સ્વતંત્રતા અને ગૌરવ સુધી બદલાઈ.

✅ Detailed Answer

It transformed him from a frightened young man into a bold activist. It separated him from home and family and made the liberation of his people the central purpose of his life.

✅ Final Answer

His hunger for freedom turned him into a courageous leader who devoted his life to liberating his people.

🌐 ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: સ્વતંત્રતાની તીવ્ર ઇચ્છાએ તેમને પોતાના લોકોની મુક્તિ માટે જીવન સમર્પિત કરનાર સાહસી નેતા બનાવ્યા.
