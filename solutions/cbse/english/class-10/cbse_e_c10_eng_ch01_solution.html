Chapter 1: A Letter to God

BoardCBSE
Class10
SubjectEnglish
MediumEnglish
Chapter NameA Letter to God
Included only: Before You Read in-text questions, all Oral Comprehension Checks, and Thinking about the Text. Activities, language exercises, speaking, listening, writing, and poems are excluded.

PART I — In-text Questions (Inside Chapter)

Before You Read

✅ Answer

Literally, the letter does not reach God. It reaches the post office, where the postman and postmaster read it. However, the postmaster decides to preserve Lencho’s faith and responds to the letter on God’s behalf.

Key Point

The story uses this situation to explore faith, human kindness, and irony.

✅ Final Answer

The letter reaches the post office, and the postmaster acts as God’s helper by answering it.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: પત્ર પોસ્ટ ઓફિસ સુધી પહોંચે છે અને પોસ્ટમાસ્ટર ભગવાનના સહાયક તરીકે તેનો જવાબ આપે છે.

✅ Answer

No supernatural event occurs. The postmaster collects money from his employees, contributes part of his own salary, and asks friends to contribute. He then sends seventy pesos to Lencho in an envelope signed “God”.

✅ Final Answer

The money is sent by the postmaster and his helpers, not directly by God.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: પૈસા સીધા ભગવાન મોકલતા નથી; પોસ્ટમાસ્ટર અને તેના સહયોગીઓ મોકલે છે.

Oral Comprehension Check — 1

✅ Answer

Lencho hoped for a downpour or at least a good shower because his ripe cornfield needed water for a successful harvest.

✅ Final Answer

He hoped for rain to nourish his crop.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તેણે પાકને પાણી મળે તે માટે વરસાદની આશા રાખી હતી.

✅ Answer

Rain promised a good harvest, and a good harvest would bring money. Therefore, Lencho compared the large drops to ten-cent coins and the smaller drops to five-cent coins.

✅ Final Answer

He called them “new coins” because the rain would turn his crop into income.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: વરસાદ સારો પાક અને કમાણી લાવશે, તેથી તેણે ટીપાંને નવા સિક્કા કહ્યું.

✅ Answer

The pleasant rain changed into a violent hailstorm. Large hailstones fell for about an hour, covering the valley like salt and completely destroying the corn, flowers, and leaves.

✅ Final Answer

The rain became a hailstorm and ruined Lencho’s entire crop.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: વરસાદ કરાવર્ષામાં બદલાયો અને લેન્ચોનો આખો પાક નષ્ટ થયો.

✅ Answer

Lencho was filled with deep sadness and despair. He realised that the hail had destroyed everything and that his family might go hungry because there would be no corn that year.

✅ Final Answer

He felt heartbroken, hopeless, and worried about his family’s survival.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તે ખૂબ દુઃખી, નિરાશ અને પરિવારના જીવનનિર્વાહ અંગે ચિંતિત થયો.

Oral Comprehension Check — 2

✅ Answer

Lencho had complete faith in God. Believing that God could see everything and would help him, he wrote a letter asking for one hundred pesos to sow his field again and support his family until the next crop.

✅ Final Answer

He trusted God completely and wrote to Him asking for one hundred pesos.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તેને ભગવાન પર સંપૂર્ણ વિશ્વાસ હતો અને તેણે સો પેસોની મદદ માગતો પત્ર લખ્યો.

✅ Answer

A postman first noticed the unusual address “To God” and showed the letter to the postmaster. The postmaster then read it.

✅ Final Answer

The postmaster read Lencho’s letter after the postman showed it to him.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: પોસ્ટમૅને પત્ર બતાવ્યા પછી પોસ્ટમાસ્ટરે લેન્ચોનો પત્ર વાંચ્યો.

✅ Answer

The postmaster decided to answer the letter so that Lencho’s faith would not be shaken. He collected money from employees, donated part of his salary, and asked friends to contribute. He sent seventy pesos with a note signed “God”.

✅ Final Answer

He collected money and sent it to Lencho in God’s name.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તેણે પૈસા એકત્રિત કરીને ભગવાનના નામે લેન્ચોને મોકલ્યા.

Oral Comprehension Check — 3

✅ Answer

No. Lencho had such complete confidence in God that he expected his request to be answered. Therefore, receiving the money did not surprise him at all.

✅ Final Answer

No, he was not surprised because his faith in God was absolute.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: નહીં, ભગવાન પર સંપૂર્ણ વિશ્વાસ હોવાથી તેને આશ્ચર્ય થયું નહીં.

✅ Answer

Lencho had asked for one hundred pesos but received only seventy. Since he believed God could neither make a mistake nor deny his full request, he assumed that the post office employees had stolen the remaining thirty pesos.

✅ Final Answer

He became angry because thirty pesos were missing.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: ત્રીસ પેસો ઓછા હોવાથી તે ગુસ્સે થયો.

PART II — Thinking about the Text

✅ Answer

Lencho has complete faith in God. His belief is shown when he thinks that God sees everything, even what lies deep in a person’s conscience, and when he confidently writes directly to God asking for one hundred pesos.

“All through the night, Lencho thought only of his one hope: the help of God…”

“God… if you don’t help me, my family and I will go hungry this year.”

His lack of surprise on receiving money further proves the strength of his faith.

✅ Final Answer

Lencho has complete faith in God, as shown by his letter and his certainty that God would help him.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: લેન્ચોને ભગવાન પર સંપૂર્ણ વિશ્વાસ છે, જે તેના પત્ર અને ભગવાન મદદ કરશે તેવી તેની ખાતરીથી સ્પષ્ટ થાય છે.

✅ Answer

The postmaster is deeply impressed by Lencho’s unquestioning faith. He sends money because he does not want that faith to be shaken. He signs the letter “God” so that Lencho will believe the help has truly come from God and his faith will remain intact.

Character Insight

The postmaster is compassionate, generous, and sensitive to another person’s beliefs.

✅ Final Answer

He sends the money to preserve Lencho’s faith and signs “God” to make the help believable.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તે લેન્ચોનો વિશ્વાસ જળવાઈ રહે તે માટે પૈસા મોકલે છે અને મદદ વિશ્વસનીય લાગે તે માટે “ભગવાન” તરીકે સહી કરે છે.

✅ Answer

No. Lencho never tried to identify the sender because he had no doubt that the money came from God. His faith was so complete that he did not consider any human explanation.

✅ Final Answer

No; he believed without question that God had sent the money.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: નહીં; તેને નિઃશંક વિશ્વાસ હતો કે પૈસા ભગવાને મોકલ્યા હતા.

✅ Answer

Lencho thinks the post office employees have stolen the missing thirty pesos. The irony is that these very employees, especially the postmaster, had collected and donated the money out of kindness. Instead of thanking them, Lencho calls them “a bunch of crooks”.

Irony

The helpers are mistaken for thieves, while Lencho remains unaware of their generosity.

✅ Final Answer

He blames the post office employees, although they are actually the people who helped him.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: તે પોસ્ટ ઓફિસના કર્મચારીઓને દોષ આપે છે, જ્યારે વાસ્તવમાં તેમણે જ તેની મદદ કરી હતી.

✅ Answer

Yes, people like Lencho can exist in the real world. He is best described as naive, unquestioning, and to some extent ungrateful. His faith is sincere but so absolute that he fails to understand human kindness. Calling his benefactors “crooks” makes his behaviour unintentionally comical.

It would be unfair to call him simply stupid or greedy because his demand arose from genuine need after the destruction of his crop.

✅ Final Answer

Lencho is naive, unquestioning, and ungrateful, though his faith and need are genuine.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: લેન્ચો નિર્દોષ, પ્રશ્ન ન કરનાર અને કૃતઘ્ન છે, છતાં તેનો વિશ્વાસ અને જરૂરિયાત સાચી છે.

✅ Answer

Humans versus nature: Lencho depends on rain for his crop, but the rain turns into a destructive hailstorm and ruins his field. This shows how powerless human beings can be before natural forces.

Humans versus humans: The postmaster and his employees help Lencho generously, yet Lencho suspects them of stealing part of the money. His misunderstanding creates conflict between him and the very people who helped him.

✅ Final Answer

The hailstorm represents conflict with nature, while Lencho’s suspicion of the postal employees represents conflict among humans.

ગુજરાતી અનુવાદ: કરાવર્ષા માનવ અને પ્રકૃતિનો સંઘર્ષ દર્શાવે છે, જ્યારે પોસ્ટ કર્મચારીઓ પરનો લેન્ચોનો શંકા માનવો વચ્ચેનો સંઘર્ષ દર્શાવે છે.
