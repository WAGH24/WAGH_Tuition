Important Formulas (Master Sheet)

Reflection & Spherical Mirrors

Radius & Focal Length: R = 2f
Mirror Formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f
Magnification (Mirror): m = h'/h = -v/u

Refraction & Spherical Lenses

Snell's Law: sin(i) / sin(r) = constant
Absolute Refractive Index: nm = c / v
Relative Refractive Index: n21 = v1 / v2
Lens Formula: 1/v - 1/u = 1/f
Magnification (Lens): m = h'/h = v/u
Power of a Lens: P = 1/f (in meters)

Core Concepts and Key Words

1. Reflection of Light

  • Laws of Reflection: The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection ($i = r$). The incident ray, normal, and reflected ray all lie in the same plane.
  • Spherical Mirrors: A concave mirror has its reflecting surface curved inwards. A convex mirror has its reflecting surface curved outwards.
  • Principal Focus (F): The point on the principal axis where light rays parallel to the principal axis converge (concave) or appear to diverge from (convex).

2. Refraction of Light

  • Refraction: The bending of light as it passes from one transparent medium to another due to a change in the speed of light.
  • Refractive Index (n): The ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum (c) to the speed of light in the medium (v).
  • Optical Density: A medium with a higher refractive index is optically denser, slowing down light more than a rarer medium.

3. Spherical Lenses

  • Convex Lens: Thicker at the middle; converges light rays.
  • Concave Lens: Thicker at the edges; diverges light rays.
  • Power of a Lens (P): The degree of convergence or divergence. Measured in Dioptres (D).

Detailed Analysis of Chapter Activities (9.1 to 9.13)

Activity 9.1: Reflecting Surfaces of a Spoon

Procedure: View your face in the curved surface of a large shining spoon. Move it away and observe. Reverse the spoon and repeat.

Observation & Conclusion: The inner curve acts as a concave mirror (image gets inverted when moved away). The outer curve acts as a convex mirror (image remains erect, virtual, and diminished).

Activity 9.2: Finding Focal Length of a Concave Mirror

Procedure: Direct a concave mirror towards the Sun. Focus the reflected light onto a sheet of paper until a sharp bright spot forms.

Observation & Conclusion: The focused sunlight produces immense heat, igniting the paper. The distance between the mirror and the sharp spot gives the approximate focal length of the concave mirror.

Activity 9.3: Image Formation by Concave Mirror

Procedure: Mount a concave mirror on a stand. Place a lighted candle on an optical bench at various positions (Beyond C, At C, Between C & F, At F, Between P & F) and try to catch the image on a screen.

Observation & Conclusion: As the object moves closer to the mirror from infinity to F, the real, inverted image moves further away and enlarges. When placed between P and F, the image cannot be caught on a screen as it becomes virtual, erect, and magnified behind the mirror.

Activity 9.4: Ray Diagrams for Concave Mirror

Procedure: Draw neat ray diagrams for the image formation positions observed in Activity 9.3.

Conclusion: Tracing at least two standard incident rays (one parallel to the principal axis, and one passing through the focus or centre of curvature) accurately predicts the size, position, and nature of the image formed.

Activity 9.5: Image Formation by a Convex Mirror

Procedure: Hold a convex mirror and observe the image of an upright pencil. Move the pencil away slowly and observe the changes in the image.

Observation & Conclusion: The image is always erect, virtual, and diminished. As the object recedes, the image becomes smaller and shifts closer to the principal focus behind the mirror.

Activity 9.6: Field of View of Mirrors

Procedure: Try viewing the full-length image of a distant tall building or tree in a plane mirror, a concave mirror, and a convex mirror.

Observation & Conclusion: A plane mirror and a concave mirror fail to show the full height. Only the convex mirror provides a wide enough field of view to see the full-length image, explaining its use as a rear-view mirror in vehicles.

Activity 9.7: Refraction - The Disappearing Coin

Procedure: Place a coin at the bottom of an empty bowl. Move back just until the coin disappears from your line of sight. Ask a friend to gently pour water into the bowl.

Observation & Conclusion: The coin becomes visible again. This happens because of refraction; light travelling from denser water to rarer air bends away from the normal, making the coin appear raised.

Activity 9.8: Refraction - Glass Slab over Text

Procedure: Place a thick glass slab over a printed page or draw a thick straight ink line and place the glass slab over it.

Observation & Conclusion: The letters or line appear raised when viewed through the slab. This is a direct consequence of the refraction of light through the glass.

Activity 9.9: Refraction through a Rectangular Glass Slab

Procedure: Draw the outline of a glass slab on paper. Use drawing pins to trace an incident ray. Look through the opposite side to align more pins with the emergent ray.

Observation & Conclusion: The emergent ray is parallel to the incident ray but is laterally displaced. Light bends towards the normal upon entering the denser glass and away from the normal upon exiting into the rarer air.

Activity 9.10: Finding Focal Length of a Convex Lens

Procedure: Hold a convex lens facing the Sun. Focus the transmitted light onto a sheet of paper until a sharp bright spot appears.

Observation & Conclusion: The lens converges sunlight to a point on the other side (principal focus). The distance from the optical centre of the lens to the paper is its approximate focal length.

Activity 9.11: Image Formation by a Convex Lens

Procedure: Mount a convex lens on an optical bench. Place a lighted candle at various distances (Beyond 2F, At 2F, Between F & 2F, At F, Between F & O) and try to catch the image on a screen on the opposite side.

Observation & Conclusion: As the object approaches the lens up to F, the real, inverted image moves further away and becomes enlarged. When placed between F and the optical centre, no real image is formed; instead, a virtual, erect, and magnified image can be seen by looking into the lens.

Activity 9.12: Image Formation by a Concave Lens

Procedure: Replace the convex lens with a concave lens on the optical bench. Move the candle to various positions and observe the image.

Observation & Conclusion: A concave lens will always form a virtual, erect, and diminished image, irrespective of the object's position. The image always lies between the optical centre and the principal focus on the same side as the object.

Activity 9.13: Identifying Lenses and Mirrors

Procedure: Analyze various lenses and mirrors given to you by touching their surfaces and observing the nature of the image (magnification) when placed close to an object (like printed text).

Observation & Conclusion:
• Bulging outwards / Magnifies text when close: Convex Lens / Concave Mirror
• Curved inwards / Shrinks text: Concave Lens / Convex Mirror
This confirms the functional optical properties tied to the physical shape of the elements.

Set 1

Q1. Define the principal focus of a concave mirror.▼ Tap to View Solution

✅ Answer
Light rays parallel to the principal axis of a concave mirror converge at a fixed point on the principal axis after reflection. This point is called the principal focus.
ગુજરાતી: અવતલ દર્પણના મુખ્ય અક્ષને સમાંતર આવતા પ્રકાશ કિરણો પરાવર્તન પછી મુખ્ય અક્ષ પરના એક ચોક્કસ બિંદુએ ભેગા થાય છે. આ બિંદુને મુખ્ય ફોકસ કહે છે.

Q2. The radius of curvature of a spherical mirror is 20 cm. What is its focal length?▼ Tap to View Solution

📋 Given
\(R = 20\ \text{cm}\)
📘 Formula Used
\(f=\dfrac{R}{2}\)
✏️ Solution
\(f=\dfrac{20}{2}=10\ \text{cm}\)
✅ Focal length = 10 cm.
ગુજરાતી: આપેલ છે R = 20 cm. f = R/2 = 20/2 = 10 cm.

Q3. Name a mirror that can give an erect and enlarged image of an object.▼ Tap to View Solution

✅ Answer
A concave mirror can give an erect and enlarged image when the object is placed between the pole and principal focus.
ગુજરાતી: વસ્તુ ધ્રુવ અને મુખ્ય ફોકસ વચ્ચે હોય ત્યારે અવતલ દર્પણ સીધું અને મોટું પ્રતિબિંબ આપે છે.

Q4. Why do we prefer a convex mirror as a rear-view mirror in vehicles?▼ Tap to View Solution

✅ Answer
Convex mirrors always form erect and diminished images. They also provide a wider field of view, so the driver can see a larger area behind the vehicle.
ગુજરાતી: બહિર્ગોળ દર્પણ હંમેશાં સીધું અને નાનું પ્રતિબિંબ આપે છે. તે વિશાળ દૃશ્યક્ષેત્ર આપે છે, તેથી વાહન પાછળનો મોટો વિસ્તાર જોઈ શકાય છે.

Q2. A concave mirror produces three times magnified real image of an object placed at 10 cm in front of it. Where is the image located?▼ Tap to View Solution

📋 Given
\(m=-3,\quad u=-10\ \text{cm}\)
📘 Formula Used
\(m=-\dfrac{v}{u}\)
✏️ Solution
\(-3=-\dfrac{v}{-10}\)\(-3=\dfrac{v}{10}\)\(v=-30\ \text{cm}\)

Set 3

Q1. A ray of light travelling in air enters obliquely into water. Does the ray bend towards the normal or away from the normal? Why?▼ Tap to View Solution

✅ Answer
The ray bends towards the normal because water is optically denser than air. When light enters a denser medium, its speed decreases and it bends towards the normal.
ગુજરાતી: કિરણ અભિલંબ તરફ વળે છે, કારણ કે પાણી હવા કરતાં પ્રકાશીય રીતે વધુ ઘન છે.

Q2. Light enters from air to glass having refractive index 1.50. What is the speed of light in glass? Speed of light in vacuum is \(3\times10^8\ \text{m/s}\).▼ Tap to View Solution

📋 Given
\(n=1.50,\quad c=3\times10^8\ \text{m/s}\)
📘 Formula Used
\(n=\dfrac{c}{v}\quad \Rightarrow \quad v=\dfrac{c}{n}\)
✏️ Solution
\(v=\dfrac{3\times10^8}{1.50}=2\times10^8\ \text{m/s}\)
✅ Speed of light in glass = \(2\times10^8\ \text{m/s}\).
ગુજરાતી: n = c/v એટલે v = c/n = (3 × 10⁸)/1.50 = 2 × 10⁸ m/s.

Q3. Find out, from the table, the medium having highest optical density. Also find the medium with lowest optical density.▼ Tap to View Solution

✅ Answer
Highest optical density: Diamond (refractive index 2.42).
Lowest optical density: Air (refractive index 1.0003).
ગુજરાતી: સૌથી વધુ પ્રકાશીય ઘનતા: હીરો. સૌથી ઓછી પ્રકાશીય ઘનતા: હવા.

Q4. You are given kerosene, turpentine and water. In which of these does light travel fastest?▼ Tap to View Solution

✅ Answer
Light travels fastest in the medium having the lowest refractive index. Among water, kerosene and turpentine, water has the lowest refractive index. Therefore, light travels fastest in water.
ગુજરાતી: પ્રકાશ સૌથી ઓછા વક્રીભવનાંકવાળા માધ્યમમાં સૌથી ઝડપી ચાલે છે. તેથી પ્રકાશ પાણીમાં સૌથી ઝડપી ચાલે છે.

Q5. The refractive index of diamond is 2.42. What is the meaning of this statement?▼ Tap to View Solution

✅ Answer
It means that the speed of light in diamond is \(1/2.42\) times the speed of light in vacuum. In other words, light travels 2.42 times slower in diamond than in vacuum.
ગુજરાતી: તેનો અર્થ એ છે કે હીરામાં પ્રકાશની ઝડપ નિર્વાતમાં પ્રકાશની ઝડપની 1/2.42 ગણી છે.

Set 4

Q1. Define 1 dioptre of power of a lens.▼ Tap to View Solution

✅ Answer
One dioptre is the power of a lens whose focal length is 1 metre.
\(1\ \text{D}=1\ \text{m}^{-1}\)
ગુજરાતી: 1 ડાયોપ્ટર એ એવા લેન્સની શક્તિ છે જેની ફોકલ લંબાઈ 1 મીટર હોય છે.

Q2. A convex lens forms a real and inverted image of a needle at a distance of 50 cm from it. Where is the needle placed if the image is equal to the size of the object? Also find the power of the lens.▼ Tap to View Solution

📋 Given
\(v=+50\ \text{cm},\quad m=-1\)
✏️ Find object distance
\(m=\dfrac{v}{u}\)\(-1=\dfrac{50}{u}\)\(u=-50\ \text{cm}\)
✏️ Find focal length
\(\dfrac{1}{f}=\dfrac{1}{v}-\dfrac{1}{u}\)\(\dfrac{1}{f}=\dfrac{1}{50}-\dfrac{1}{-50}=\dfrac{2}{50}=\dfrac{1}{25}\)\(f=+25\ \text{cm}=+0.25\ \text{m}\)
✏️ Find power
\(P=\dfrac{1}{f}=\dfrac{1}{0.25}=+4.0\ \text{D}\)
✅ Needle is placed 50 cm in front of the lens. Power = +4.0 D.
ગુજરાતી: u = -50 cm. f = +25 cm = +0.25 m. P = +4.0 D.

Q3. Find the power of a concave lens of focal length 2 m.▼ Tap to View Solution

📋 Given
\(f=-2\ \text{m}\)
📘 Formula Used
\(P=\dfrac{1}{f}\)
✏️ Solution
\(P=\dfrac{1}{-2}=-0.5\ \text{D}\)
✅ Power = −0.5 D.
ગુજરાતી: અવતલ લેન્સ માટે f = -2 m. P = 1/f = -0.5 D.

Q7. We wish to obtain an erect image of an object using a concave mirror of focal length 15 cm. What should be the range of distance of the object from the mirror?▼ Tap to View Solution

ગુજરાતી: વસ્તુ ધ્રુવ અને ફોકસ વચ્ચે એટલે 0 થી 15 cm વચ્ચે રાખવી જોઈએ.

Q9. One-half of a convex lens is covered with a black paper. Will this lens produce a complete image of the object? Explain.▼ Tap to View Solution

✅ Answer
Yes, it will produce a complete image. Rays from every point of the object can still pass through the uncovered part of the lens and form the image. However, the image will be less bright because less light reaches the screen.
ગુજરાતી: હા, સંપૂર્ણ પ્રતિબિંબ બનશે, પરંતુ તેની તેજસ્વિતા ઓછી થશે કારણ કે પ્રકાશ ઓછો પહોંચે છે.

Q10. An object 5 cm in length is held 25 cm away from a converging lens of focal length 10 cm. Find the position, size and nature of the image.▼ Tap to View Solution

📋 Given
\(h=+5\ \text{cm},\quad u=-25\ \text{cm},\quad f=+10\ \text{cm}\)
📘 Formula Used
\(\dfrac{1}{v}-\dfrac{1}{u}=\dfrac{1}{f}\)
✏️ Find image distance
\(\dfrac{1}{v}-\dfrac{1}{-25}=\dfrac{1}{10}\)\(\dfrac{1}{v}+\dfrac{1}{25}=\dfrac{1}{10}\)\(\dfrac{1}{v}=\dfrac{1}{10}-\dfrac{1}{25}=\dfrac{5-2}{50}=\dfrac{3}{50}\)\(v=\dfrac{50}{3}=+16.67\ \text{cm}\)
✏️ Find image height
\(m=\dfrac{v}{u}=\dfrac{50/3}{-25}=-\dfrac{2}{3}\)\(h'=m\times h=-\dfrac{2}{3}\times5=-3.33\ \text{cm}\)
✅ Final Answer
QuantityAnswer
Position16.67 cm behind the lens
Size3.33 cm
NatureReal, inverted and diminished
ગુજરાતી: પ્રતિબિંબ લેન્સની પાછળ 16.67 cm પર બને છે. h′ નકારાત્મક હોવાથી પ્રતિબિંબ ઊલટું છે. તેથી પ્રતિબિંબ વાસ્તવિક, ઊલટું અને નાનું છે.

Q11. A concave lens of focal length 15 cm forms an image 10 cm from the lens. How far is the object placed from the lens?▼ Tap to View Solution

📋 Given
\(f=-15\ \text{cm},\quad v=-10\ \text{cm}\)
\(\dfrac{1}{v}-\dfrac{1}{u}=\dfrac{1}{f}\)
✏️ Solution
\(\dfrac{1}{-10}-\dfrac{1}{u}=\dfrac{1}{-15}\)\(\dfrac{1}{u}=-\dfrac{1}{10}+\dfrac{1}{15}=-\dfrac{1}{30}\)\(u=-30\ \text{cm}\)
