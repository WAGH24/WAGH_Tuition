In-text Question Set 1Spherical Mirrors

✅ Answer

The principal focus of a concave mirror is the point on its principal axis at which rays parallel to the principal axis converge after reflection.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Consider a beam of light whose rays are parallel to the principal axis.
  2. After striking the concave mirror, the rays are reflected inward.
  3. The reflected rays meet at one point on the principal axis.
  4. This point is represented by F and is called the principal focus.
✅ Final Answer
The principal focus is the point on the principal axis where rays parallel to the axis meet after reflection from a concave mirror.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
અવતલ અરીસાનું મુખ્ય કેન્દ્રબિંદુ એ મુખ્ય અક્ષ પરનું બિંદુ છે જ્યાં મુખ્ય અક્ષને સમાંતર કિરણો પરાવર્તન પછી ભેગાં થાય છે.
📋 Given

Radius of curvature: \(R=20\,\text{cm}\)

📘 Formula Used
\[R=2f\quad\Rightarrow\quad f=\frac{R}{2}\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

Substitute \(R=20\,\text{cm}\):

\[f=\frac{20}{2}\]
\[f=10\,\text{cm}\]

The magnitude of the focal length is 10 cm. Under the Cartesian sign convention it is \(-10\,\text{cm}\) for a concave mirror and \(+10\,\text{cm}\) for a convex mirror.

✅ Final Answer
\(f=10\,\text{cm}\) in magnitude.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
કેન્દ્રવક્રતાની ત્રિજ્યા 20 cm હોય તો કેન્દ્રલંબાઈનું પરિમાણ \(10\,\text{cm}\) છે.
✅ Answer

A concave mirror can form an erect and enlarged image.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Place the object between the pole \(P\) and the principal focus \(F\) of a concave mirror.
  2. The reflected rays diverge.
  3. Their backward extensions meet behind the mirror.
  4. The resulting image is virtual, erect and enlarged.
✅ Final Answer
A concave mirror, when the object is placed between its pole and principal focus.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
વસ્તુને ધ્રુવ અને મુખ્ય કેન્દ્રબિંદુ વચ્ચે મૂકવામાં આવે ત્યારે અવતલ અરીસો સીધું અને મોટું પ્રતિબિંબ આપે છે.
✅ Answer

A convex mirror gives a wide field of view and always forms a virtual, erect and diminished image.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. The outward-curved reflecting surface diverges the reflected rays.
  2. This allows the mirror to cover a larger area behind the vehicle than a plane mirror of the same size.
  3. The image is always erect, so the driver sees vehicles in their normal orientation.
  4. The image is diminished, allowing several vehicles and a larger road area to be seen at once.
✅ Final Answer
Convex mirrors are used because they provide a wider field of view and form erect, diminished images of traffic behind the vehicle.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
બહિર્ગોળ અરીસો વિશાળ દૃશ્યક્ષેત્ર આપે છે અને પાછળના વાહનોનાં સીધાં તથા નાનાં પ્રતિબિંબ બનાવે છે, તેથી તેને રિયર-વ્યૂ મિરર તરીકે પસંદ કરવામાં આવે છે.

In-text Question Set 3Refraction and Refractive Index

✅ Answer

The ray bends towards the normal.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Air is optically rarer than water.
  2. When light enters water, its speed decreases.
  3. A ray entering an optically denser medium obliquely bends towards the normal.
✅ Final Answer
The ray bends towards the normal because it travels from optically rarer air into optically denser water and slows down.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
કિરણ લંબ તરફ વળે છે, કારણ કે તે પ્રકાશીય રીતે વિરલ હવામાંથી પ્રકાશીય રીતે ઘન પાણીમાં પ્રવેશે છે અને તેની ઝડપ ઘટે છે.
📋 Given

Refractive index: \(n=1.50\)

Speed of light in vacuum: \(c=3\times10^8\,\text{m s}^{-1}\)

📘 Formula Used
\[n=\frac{c}{v}\quad\Rightarrow\quad v=\frac{c}{n}\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
\[v=\frac{3\times10^8}{1.50}\]
\[v=2\times10^8\,\text{m s}^{-1}\]
✅ Final Answer
The speed of light in glass is \(2.0\times10^8\,\text{m s}^{-1}\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
કાચમાં પ્રકાશની ઝડપ \(2.0\times10^8\,\text{m s}^{-1}\) છે.
📘 Key Principle

A medium with a larger refractive index is optically denser.

✏️ Step-by-Step Comparison
  1. From Table 9.3, diamond has the largest refractive index: \(2.42\).
  2. Air has the smallest refractive index: \(1.0003\).
  3. Therefore, diamond has the highest optical density and air has the lowest optical density.
✅ Final Answer
Highest optical density: diamond. Lowest optical density: air.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
સૌથી વધુ પ્રકાશીય ઘનતા: હીરો. સૌથી ઓછી પ્રકાશીય ઘનતા: હવા.
📋 Refractive Indices

Water: \(1.33\)

Kerosene: \(1.44\)

Turpentine oil: \(1.47\)

📘 Relation
\[v=\frac{c}{n}\]

For the same value of \(c\), a smaller refractive index means a greater speed.

✏️ Step-by-Step Reasoning
  1. Compare the three refractive indices.
  2. Water has the smallest value, \(1.33\).
  3. Therefore light travels fastest in water.
✅ Final Answer
Light travels fastest in water.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
પ્રકાશ પાણીમાં સૌથી વધુ ઝડપથી પ્રવાસ કરે છે.
📘 Definition
\[n_{\text{diamond}}=\frac{c}{v_{\text{diamond}}}=2.42\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Meaning
  1. The ratio of the speed of light in vacuum to its speed in diamond is 2.42.
  2. Therefore, light travels 2.42 times faster in vacuum than in diamond.
  3. The speed in diamond is:
\[v_{\text{diamond}}=\frac{3\times10^8}{2.42}\approx1.24\times10^8\,\text{m s}^{-1}\]
✅ Final Answer
It means \(c/v_{\text{diamond}}=2.42\); light travels in diamond at about \(1.24\times10^8\,\text{m s}^{-1}\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
તેનો અર્થ \(c/v_{\text{diamond}}=2.42\) છે; હીરામાં પ્રકાશની ઝડપ આશરે \(1.24\times10^8\,\text{m s}^{-1}\) છે.

In-text Question Set 4Power of a Lens

✅ Answer

One dioptre is the power of a lens whose focal length is one metre.

📘 Formula
\[P=\frac{1}{f}\]
✏️ Explanation

If \(f=1\,\text{m}\), then:

\[P=\frac{1}{1\,\text{m}}=1\,\text{m}^{-1}=1\,\text{D}\]
✅ Final Answer
One dioptre is the power of a lens of focal length \(1\,\text{m}\); \(1\,\text{D}=1\,\text{m}^{-1}\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
1 ડાયોપ્ટર એ \(1\,\text{m}\) કેન્દ્રલંબાઈ ધરાવતા લેન્સની શક્તિ છે; \(1\,\text{D}=1\,\text{m}^{-1}\).
📋 Given

Image distance: \(v=+50\,\text{cm}\)

Real image equal in size to object: \(m=-1\)

📘 Formulae Used
\[m=\frac{v}{u}\]
\[\frac{1}{v}-\frac{1}{u}=\frac{1}{f}\]
\[P=\frac{1}{f(\text{m})}\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1: Find object distance.

\[-1=\frac{50}{u}\]
\[u=-50\,\text{cm}\]

The needle is 50 cm in front of the lens.

Step 2: Find focal length.

\[\frac{1}{f}=\frac{1}{50}-\frac{1}{-50}\]
\[\frac{1}{f}=\frac{2}{50}=\frac{1}{25}\]
\[f=25\,\text{cm}=0.25\,\text{m}\]

Step 3: Find power.

\[P=\frac{1}{0.25}=+4\,\text{D}\]
✅ Final Answer
The needle is placed \(50\,\text{cm}\) in front of the lens. The lens has focal length \(25\,\text{cm}\) and power \(+4\,\text{D}\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
સોય લેન્સની સામે \(50\,\text{cm}\) અંતરે મૂકાય છે. લેન્સની કેન્દ્રલંબાઈ \(25\,\text{cm}\) અને શક્તિ \(+4\,\text{D}\) છે.
📋 Given

For a concave lens: \(f=-2\,\text{m}\)

📘 Formula Used
\[P=\frac{1}{f}\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
\[P=\frac{1}{-2}\]
\[P=-0.5\,\text{D}\]
✅ Final Answer
The power of the concave lens is \(-0.5\,\text{D}\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
અંતર્ગોળ લેન્સની શક્તિ \(-0.5\,\text{D}\) છે.

\[\frac{1}{v}+\frac{1}{u}=\frac{1}{f}\]
\[m=-\frac{v}{u}=\frac{h'}{h}\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1: Focal length.

\[f=\frac{30}{2}=+15\,\text{cm}\]

Step 2: Image distance.

\[\frac{1}{v}+\frac{1}{-20}=\frac{1}{15}\]
\[\frac{1}{v}=\frac{1}{15}+\frac{1}{20}=\frac{7}{60}\]
\[v=\frac{60}{7}\approx+8.57\,\text{cm}\]

Step 3: Image size.

\[m=-\frac{8.57}{-20}\approx+0.429\]
\[h'=mh=(0.429)(5.0)\approx+2.14\,\text{cm}\]
✅ Final Answer
The image is about \(8.57\,\text{cm}\) behind the mirror, virtual and erect, with height about \(2.14\,\text{cm}\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
પ્રતિબિંબ અરીસાની પાછળ આશરે \(8.57\,\text{cm}\) અંતરે બને છે. તે આભાસી અને સીધું છે તથા તેનું કદ આશરે \(2.14\,\text{cm}\) છે.
📋 Given

Object height: \(h=+7.0\,\text{cm}\)

Object distance: \(u=-27\,\text{cm}\)

Concave mirror focal length: \(f=-18\,\text{cm}\)

📘 Formulae Used
\[\frac{1}{v}+\frac{1}{u}=\frac{1}{f}\]
\[m=-\frac{v}{u}=\frac{h'}{h}\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1: Find image distance.

\[\frac{1}{v}+\frac{1}{-27}=\frac{1}{-18}\]
\[\frac{1}{v}=-\frac{1}{18}+\frac{1}{27}\]
\[\frac{1}{v}=\frac{-3+2}{54}=-\frac{1}{54}\]
\[v=-54\,\text{cm}\]

The screen must be placed 54 cm in front of the mirror.

Step 2: Find magnification and height.

\[m=-\frac{-54}{-27}=-2\]
\[h'=mh=(-2)(7.0)=-14\,\text{cm}\]

The negative height shows an inverted image.

✅ Final Answer
Place the screen \(54\,\text{cm}\) in front of the mirror. The image is real, inverted and enlarged, with size \(14\,\text{cm}\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
સ્ક્રીન અરીસાની સામે \(54\,\text{cm}\) અંતરે મૂકવી. પ્રતિબિંબ વાસ્તવિક, ઊંધું અને મોટું છે તથા તેનું કદ \(14\,\text{cm}\) છે.
📋 Given

Power: \(P=-2.0\,\text{D}\)

📘 Formula Used
\[P=\frac{1}{f}\quad\Rightarrow\quad f=\frac{1}{P}\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
\[f=\frac{1}{-2.0}\,\text{m}\]
\[f=-0.50\,\text{m}=-50\,\text{cm}\]

A negative focal length identifies a concave (diverging) lens.

✅ Final Answer
The focal length is \(-0.50\,\text{m}\) or \(-50\,\text{cm}\). It is a concave lens.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
કેન્દ્રલંબાઈ \(-0.50\,\text{m}\) અથવા \(-50\,\text{cm}\) છે. આ અંતર્ગોળ લેન્સ છે.
📋 Given

Power: \(P=+1.5\,\text{D}\)

📘 Formula Used
\[f=\frac{1}{P}\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
\[f=\frac{1}{1.5}\,\text{m}\]
\[f\approx+0.667\,\text{m}\]
\[f\approx+66.7\,\text{cm}\]

Positive power and focal length indicate a convex, converging lens.

✅ Final Answer
The focal length is about \(+0.667\,\text{m}\) or \(+66.7\,\text{cm}\). The prescribed lens is converging (convex).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
