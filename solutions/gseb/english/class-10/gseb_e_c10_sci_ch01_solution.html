In-text Question Set 1After Chemical Equations

✅ Answer

Magnesium reacts slowly with oxygen in air and develops a thin, protective layer of magnesium oxide on its surface.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. The oxide coating prevents the magnesium metal underneath from coming into direct contact with oxygen.
  2. Rubbing the ribbon with sandpaper removes this coating.
  3. The freshly exposed magnesium then burns readily with a dazzling white flame.
  4. The product formed is magnesium oxide: \(\ce{2Mg(s) + O2(g) -> 2MgO(s)}\).
✅ Final Answer
The ribbon is cleaned to remove the magnesium oxide layer so that magnesium can burn readily in air.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
મેગ્નેશિયમ પટ્ટી પરનું મેગ્નેશિયમ ઑક્સાઇડનું આવરણ દૂર કરવા માટે તેને સાફ કરવામાં આવે છે, જેથી મેગ્નેશિયમ હવામાં સહેલાઈથી બળી શકે.
📋 Given

Three word equations are to be converted into formula equations and balanced using the smallest whole-number coefficients.

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

(i) Hydrogen + Chlorine → Hydrogen chloride

Skeletal equation: \(\ce{H2 + Cl2 -> HCl}\)

There are 2 H atoms and 2 Cl atoms on the left, but only 1 of each on the right.

Place coefficient 2 before HCl:

\[\ce{H2(g) + Cl2(g) -> 2HCl(g)}\]

(ii) Barium chloride + Aluminium sulphate → Barium sulphate + Aluminium chloride

Skeletal equation: \(\ce{BaCl2 + Al2(SO4)3 -> BaSO4 + AlCl3}\)

There are 3 sulphate groups on the left, so place 3 before \(\ce{BaSO4}\).

This requires 3 barium atoms, so place 3 before \(\ce{BaCl2}\).

There are 2 aluminium atoms on the left, so place 2 before \(\ce{AlCl3}\).

\[\ce{3BaCl2(aq) + Al2(SO4)3(aq) -> 3BaSO4(s) + 2AlCl3(aq)}\]

(iii) Sodium + Water → Sodium hydroxide + Hydrogen

Skeletal equation: \(\ce{Na + H2O -> NaOH + H2}\)

Place 2 before Na, H₂O and NaOH so that Na, H and O atoms become equal on both sides.

\[\ce{2Na(s) + 2H2O(l) -> 2NaOH(aq) + H2(g)}\]
✅ Final Answer
(i) \(\ce{H2(g) + Cl2(g) -> 2HCl(g)}\)
(ii) \(\ce{3BaCl2(aq) + Al2(SO4)3(aq) -> 3BaSO4(s) + 2AlCl3(aq)}\)
(iii) \(\ce{2Na(s) + 2H2O(l) -> 2NaOH(aq) + H2(g)}\)
🌐 Gujarati Translation
સંતુલિત સમીકરણો:
(i) \(\ce{H2(g) + Cl2(g) -> 2HCl(g)}\)
(ii) \(\ce{3BaCl2(aq) + Al2(SO4)3(aq) -> 3BaSO4(s) + 2AlCl3(aq)}\)
(iii) \(\ce{2Na(s) + 2H2O(l) -> 2NaOH(aq) + H2(g)}\)
📋 Given

The physical states must be shown: aqueous solutions as (aq), insoluble precipitate as (s), and water as (l).

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

(i) Barium chloride and sodium sulphate

Formula equation: \(\ce{BaCl2 + Na2SO4 -> BaSO4 + NaCl}\)

Balance sodium and chlorine by placing 2 before NaCl.

Barium sulphate is insoluble and appears as a white precipitate.

\[\ce{BaCl2(aq) + Na2SO4(aq) -> BaSO4(s) + 2NaCl(aq)}\]

(ii) Sodium hydroxide and hydrochloric acid

The equation already has equal numbers of Na, Cl, O and H atoms.

This is a neutralisation reaction.

\[\ce{NaOH(aq) + HCl(aq) -> NaCl(aq) + H2O(l)}\]
✅ Final Answer
(i) \(\ce{BaCl2(aq) + Na2SO4(aq) -> BaSO4(s) + 2NaCl(aq)}\)
(ii) \(\ce{NaOH(aq) + HCl(aq) -> NaCl(aq) + H2O(l)}\)
🌐 Gujarati Translation
સ્થિતિચિહ્નો સાથેના સંતુલિત સમીકરણો:
(i) \(\ce{BaCl2(aq) + Na2SO4(aq) -> BaSO4(s) + 2NaCl(aq)}\)
(ii) \(\ce{NaOH(aq) + HCl(aq) -> NaCl(aq) + H2O(l)}\)

Water is decomposed by electric current and gases are collected over the two electrodes.

  3. Blue copper sulphate changes into pale-green iron(II) sulphate.

🌐 Gujarati Translation
લોખંડ કોપર સલ્ફેટમાંથી કોપરને વિસ્થાપિત કરે છે; તેથી વાદળી દ્રાવણ ફિક્કું લીલું આયર્ન(II) સલ્ફેટ બને છે અને લોખંડ પર ભૂરું કોપર જમા થાય છે.
✅ Answer

A reaction between lead(II) nitrate and potassium iodide is a suitable example.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Lead ions exchange partners with potassium ions.
  2. Lead iodide is insoluble and appears as a yellow precipitate.
  3. Potassium nitrate remains dissolved in water.
\[\ce{Pb(NO3)2(aq) + 2KI(aq) -> PbI2(s) + 2KNO3(aq)}\]
✅ Final Answer
\(\ce{Pb(NO3)2(aq) + 2KI(aq) -> PbI2(s) + 2KNO3(aq)}\). It is a double displacement and precipitation reaction.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
\(\ce{Pb(NO3)2(aq) + 2KI(aq) -> PbI2(s) + 2KNO3(aq)}\). આ દ્વિ-વિસ્થાપન તથા અવક્ષેપન પ્રક્રિયા છે.
📘 Rule Used

Oxidation: gain of oxygen (or loss of hydrogen). Reduction: loss of oxygen (or gain of hydrogen).

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

(i) \(\ce{4Na + O2 -> 2Na2O}\)

Sodium gains oxygen, so sodium is oxidised.

Oxygen changes from elemental oxygen to oxide ions; therefore oxygen is reduced.

(ii) \(\ce{CuO + H2 -> Cu + H2O}\)

Copper(II) oxide loses oxygen and becomes copper, so \(\ce{CuO}\) is reduced.

Hydrogen gains oxygen and becomes water, so \(\ce{H2}\) is oxidised.

✅ Final Answer
(i) Sodium is oxidised; oxygen is reduced.
(ii) Hydrogen is oxidised; copper(II) oxide is reduced.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
(i) સોડિયમનું ઑક્સિડેશન થાય છે અને ઑક્સિજનનું રિડક્શન થાય છે.
(ii) હાઇડ્રોજનનું ઑક્સિડેશન થાય છે અને કોપર(II) ઑક્સાઇડનું રિડક્શન થાય છે.

✅ Answer

A balanced chemical equation has the same number of atoms of every element on the reactant side and the product side.

✏️ Explanation
  1. Atoms are neither created nor destroyed during a chemical reaction.
  2. Therefore, the total number of atoms of each element must remain unchanged.
  3. Balancing an equation makes it obey the law of conservation of mass.
  4. Only coefficients are changed; chemical formulae must never be altered.

Example: \(\ce{2H2 + O2 -> 2H2O}\). Both sides contain 4 H atoms and 2 O atoms.

✅ Final Answer
A balanced equation has equal numbers of each type of atom on both sides. It is balanced to satisfy the law of conservation of mass.

Use coefficient 2 before K, H₂O and KOH.

\[\ce{2K(s) + 2H2O(l) -> 2KOH(aq) + H2(g)}\]

✅ Answer

Exothermic reaction: a reaction that releases heat to the surroundings.

Endothermic reaction: a reaction that absorbs energy from the surroundings.

✏️ Examples

Exothermic: combustion of methane:

\[\ce{CH4(g) + 2O2(g) -> CO2(g) + 2H2O(g) + heat}\]

Endothermic: thermal decomposition of calcium carbonate:

\[\ce{CaCO3(s) ->[\Delta] CaO(s) + CO2(g)}\]
✅ Final Answer
Exothermic reactions release heat; endothermic reactions absorb energy. Combustion is exothermic, while thermal decomposition of calcium carbonate is endothermic.

✅ Answer

In a combination reaction, two or more reactants form one product. In a decomposition reaction, one reactant breaks into two or more products.

Reaction
General pattern
Example
Combination
\(\ce{A + B -> AB}\)
\(\ce{CaO + H2O -> Ca(OH)2}\)
Decomposition
\(\ce{AB -> A + B}\)
\(\ce{CaCO3 ->[\Delta] CaO + CO2}\)
✅ Final Answer
Combination joins two or more reactants into one product, whereas decomposition splits one reactant into two or more products; hence they are opposite processes.

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
  1. Write the skeletal equation: \(\ce{Cu + AgNO3 -> Cu(NO3)2 + Ag}\).
  2. Copper nitrate contains two nitrate groups, so place 2 before AgNO₃.
  3. This gives two silver atoms, so place 2 before Ag.
\[\ce{Cu(s) + 2AgNO3(aq) -> Cu(NO3)2(aq) + 2Ag(s)}\]

A precipitation reaction is a reaction in which two aqueous solutions react to form an insoluble solid called a precipitate.

✏️ Examples

Example 1: Barium sulphate precipitate

\[\ce{Na2SO4(aq) + BaCl2(aq) -> BaSO4(s) + 2NaCl(aq)}\]

\[\ce{Pb(NO3)2(aq) + 2KI(aq) -> PbI2(s) + 2KNO3(aq)}\]

\(\ce{PbI2}\) is a yellow precipitate.

✅ Final Answer
A precipitation reaction forms an insoluble solid from aqueous reactants; BaSO₄ and PbI₂ formation are examples.

📘 Definitions

Oxidation: gain of oxygen by a substance.

Reduction: loss of oxygen by a substance.

✏️ Examples

(a) Oxidation — gain of oxygen

\[\ce{2Cu + O2 -> 2CuO}\]

Copper gains oxygen.

\[\ce{2Mg + O2 -> 2MgO}\]

Magnesium gains oxygen.

(b) Reduction — loss of oxygen

\[\ce{CuO + H2 -> Cu + H2O}\]

Copper(II) oxide loses oxygen.

\[\ce{ZnO + C -> Zn + CO}\]

Zinc oxide loses oxygen.

✅ Final Answer
Oxidation is gain of oxygen; reduction is loss of oxygen. Two examples of each are shown above.

(a) Corrosion

Corrosion is the gradual damage of a metal caused by reaction with substances in its surroundings such as oxygen, moisture or acids.

Example: iron forms a reddish-brown coating of rust when exposed to moist air.

(b) Rancidity

Rancidity is the oxidation of fats and oils that produces an unpleasant smell and taste.

Example: fried food or chips left exposed to air for a long time develop a stale smell and taste.

✅ Final Answer
Corrosion is deterioration of metals by environmental reactions (for example, rusting of iron). Rancidity is oxidation of fats and oils that causes bad smell and taste.
