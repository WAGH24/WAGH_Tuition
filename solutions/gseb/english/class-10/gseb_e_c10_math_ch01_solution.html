📋 Given

The number is \(4^n\), where \(n\in\mathbb{N}\).

📘 Key Idea

A whole number ends in \(0\) only when its prime factorisation contains both \(2\) and \(5\), because \(10=2\times5\).

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1: Write \(4^n\) as a power of a prime.

\[4^n=(2^2)^n=2^{2n}\]

Step 2: The only prime factor of \(4^n\) is \(2\).

Step 3: Its prime factorisation contains no factor \(5\).

Step 4: Therefore, \(4^n\) is not divisible by \(10\).

Step 5: Hence it cannot end with the digit \(0\).

✅ Final Answer
There is no natural number \(n\) for which \(4^n\) ends with the digit \(0\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
કોઈપણ પ્રાકૃતિક સંખ્યા \(n\) માટે \(4^n\)નો અંત \(0\)થી થતો નથી, કારણ કે તેના અવિભાજ્ય અવયવીકરણમાં \(5\) અવયવ નથી.
📋 Given

Numbers: \(6\) and \(20\)

📘 Rule Used

HCF: product of the smallest powers of common prime factors.

LCM: product of the greatest powers of all prime factors involved.

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1: Prime factorise both numbers.

\[6=2\times3=2^1\times3^1\]
\[20=2\times2\times5=2^2\times5^1\]

Step 2: Find the HCF.

The only common prime factor is \(2\), and its smallest power is \(2^1\).

\[\mathrm{HCF}(6,20)=2\]

Step 3: Find the LCM.

Take \(2^2,\;3^1,\;5^1\).

\[\mathrm{LCM}(6,20)=2^2\times3\times5=60\]

Verification:

\[\mathrm{HCF}\times\mathrm{LCM}=2\times60=120=6\times20\]
✅ Final Answer
\(\mathrm{HCF}(6,20)=2\) and \(\mathrm{LCM}(6,20)=60\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
\(\mathrm{HCF}(6,20)=2\) અને \(\mathrm{LCM}(6,20)=60\).
📋 Given

Numbers: \(96\) and \(404\)

📘 Formula Used
\[\mathrm{HCF}(a,b)\times\mathrm{LCM}(a,b)=a\times b\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1: Prime factorise \(96\).

\[96=2\times2\times2\times2\times2\times3=2^5\times3\]

Step 2: Prime factorise \(404\).

\[404=4\times101=2^2\times101\]

Step 3: Find the HCF.

The common prime factor is \(2\). Its smallest power is \(2^2\).

\[\mathrm{HCF}(96,404)=2^2=4\]

Step 4: Find the LCM.

\[\mathrm{LCM}(96,404)=\frac{96\times404}{4}\]
\[=24\times404=9696\]
✅ Final Answer
\(\mathrm{HCF}(96,404)=4\) and \(\mathrm{LCM}(96,404)=9696\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
\(\mathrm{HCF}(96,404)=4\) અને \(\mathrm{LCM}(96,404)=9696\).
📋 Given

Numbers: \(6,\;72,\;120\)

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1: Prime factorise all three numbers.

\[6=2\times3\]
\[72=2^3\times3^2\]
\[120=2^3\times3\times5\]

Step 2: Find the HCF.

The common primes are \(2\) and \(3\). Their smallest powers are \(2^1\) and \(3^1\).

\[\mathrm{HCF}(6,72,120)=2\times3=6\]

Step 3: Find the LCM.

The greatest powers are \(2^3,\;3^2,\;5^1\).

\[\mathrm{LCM}(6,72,120)=2^3\times3^2\times5\]
\[=8\times9\times5=360\]

Important: For three numbers, in general,

\[\mathrm{HCF}\times\mathrm{LCM}\ne\text{product of the three numbers}\]
✅ Final Answer
\(\mathrm{HCF}(6,72,120)=6\) and \(\mathrm{LCM}(6,72,120)=360\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
\(\mathrm{HCF}(6,72,120)=6\) અને \(\mathrm{LCM}(6,72,120)=360\).
📘 Method Used

Proof by contradiction.

✏️ Step-by-Step Proof

Step 1: Assume, contrary to what we have to prove, that \(\sqrt3\) is rational.

Then it can be written in lowest terms as

\[\sqrt3=\frac{a}{b}\]

where \(a,b\) are coprime integers and \(b\ne0\).

Step 2: Square both sides.

\[3=\frac{a^2}{b^2}\]
\[a^2=3b^2\]

Thus \(3\mid a^2\). Since \(3\) is prime, \(3\mid a\).

Step 3: Let \(a=3c\) for some integer \(c\).

\[(3c)^2=3b^2\]
\[9c^2=3b^2\]
\[b^2=3c^2\]

Therefore \(3\mid b^2\), and hence \(3\mid b\).

Step 4: So \(3\) divides both \(a\) and \(b\), contradicting that \(a\) and \(b\) are coprime.

Therefore the original assumption is false.

✅ Final Answer
\(\sqrt3\) is irrational.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
\(\sqrt3\) અપરિમેય સંખ્યા છે.
📘 Method Used

Proof by contradiction, using the known fact that \(\sqrt3\) is irrational.

✏️ Step-by-Step Proof

Step 1: Assume that \(5-\sqrt3\) is rational.

Then for some integers \(a,b\), with \(b\ne0\),

\[5-\sqrt3=\frac{a}{b}\]

Step 2: Rearrange to isolate \(\sqrt3\).

\[\sqrt3=5-\frac{a}{b}\]
\[\sqrt3=\frac{5b-a}{b}\]

Step 3: Since \(a\) and \(b\) are integers, \(\frac{5b-a}{b}\) is rational.

This would make \(\sqrt3\) rational.

Step 4: But \(\sqrt3\) is irrational. This is a contradiction.

✅ Final Answer
\(5-\sqrt3\) is irrational.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
\(5-\sqrt3\) અપરિમેય સંખ્યા છે.
📘 Method Used

Proof by contradiction, using the known fact that \(\sqrt2\) is irrational.

✏️ Step-by-Step Proof

Step 1: Assume that \(3\sqrt2\) is rational.

Then for some integers \(a,b\), with \(b\ne0\),

\[3\sqrt2=\frac{a}{b}\]

Step 2: Divide both sides by \(3\).

\[\sqrt2=\frac{a}{3b}\]

Step 3: Since \(a\) and \(3b\) are integers and \(3b\ne0\), \(\frac{a}{3b}\) is rational.

This would make \(\sqrt2\) rational.

Step 4: But \(\sqrt2\) is irrational. This is a contradiction.

✅ Final Answer
\(3\sqrt2\) is irrational.

Exercise 1.1Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

(i) \(140\)

\[140=14\times10\]
\[=(2\times7)(2\times5)\]
\[=2^2\times5\times7\]

(ii) \(156\)

\[156=12\times13\]
\[=(2^2\times3)\times13\]
\[=2^2\times3\times13\]

(iii) \(3825\)

\[3825=5\times765\]
\[=5\times5\times153\]
\[=5^2\times9\times17\]
\[=3^2\times5^2\times17\]

(iv) \(5005\)

\[5005=5\times1001\]
\[=5\times7\times143\]
\[=5\times7\times11\times13\]

(v) \(7429\)

\[7429=17\times437\]
\[=17\times19\times23\]
✅ Final Answer
(i) \(140=2^2\times5\times7\)
(ii) \(156=2^2\times3\times13\)
(iii) \(3825=3^2\times5^2\times17\)
(iv) \(5005=5\times7\times11\times13\)
(v) \(7429=17\times19\times23\)
🌐 Gujarati Translation
(i) \(140=2^2\times5\times7\)
(ii) \(156=2^2\times3\times13\)
(iii) \(3825=3^2\times5^2\times17\)
(iv) \(5005=5\times7\times11\times13\)
(v) \(7429=17\times19\times23\)
📘 Formula Used
\[\mathrm{LCM}(a,b)\times\mathrm{HCF}(a,b)=a\times b\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

(i) 26 and 91

\[26=2\times13,\qquad91=7\times13\]
\[\mathrm{HCF}=13\]
\[\mathrm{LCM}=2\times7\times13=182\]
\[13\times182=2366=26\times91\]

(ii) 510 and 92

\[510=2\times3\times5\times17\]
\[92=2^2\times23\]
\[\mathrm{HCF}=2\]
\[\mathrm{LCM}=2^2\times3\times5\times17\times23=23460\]
\[2\times23460=46920=510\times92\]

(iii) 336 and 54

\[336=2^4\times3\times7\]
\[54=2\times3^3\]
\[\mathrm{HCF}=2\times3=6\]
\[\mathrm{LCM}=2^4\times3^3\times7=3024\]
\[6\times3024=18144=336\times54\]
✅ Final Answer
(i) HCF \(=13\), LCM \(=182\)
(ii) HCF \(=2\), LCM \(=23460\)
(iii) HCF \(=6\), LCM \(=3024\). In every case, \(\mathrm{HCF}\times\mathrm{LCM}\) equals the product of the two numbers.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
(i) HCF \(=13\), LCM \(=182\)
(ii) HCF \(=2\), LCM \(=23460\)
(iii) HCF \(=6\), LCM \(=3024\). દરેક કિસ્સામાં HCF × LCM બંને સંખ્યાના ગુણાકાર જેટલું થાય છે.
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

(i) 12, 15 and 21

\[12=2^2\times3,\quad15=3\times5,\quad21=3\times7\]

The common prime factor is \(3\).

\[\mathrm{HCF}=3\]
\[\mathrm{LCM}=2^2\times3\times5\times7=420\]

(ii) 17, 23 and 29

All three numbers are distinct prime numbers.

\[\mathrm{HCF}=1\]
\[\mathrm{LCM}=17\times23\times29=11339\]

(iii) 8, 9 and 25

\[8=2^3,\quad9=3^2,\quad25=5^2\]

They have no common prime factor.

\[\mathrm{HCF}=1\]
\[\mathrm{LCM}=2^3\times3^2\times5^2=1800\]
✅ Final Answer
(i) HCF \(=3\), LCM \(=420\)
(ii) HCF \(=1\), LCM \(=11339\)
(iii) HCF \(=1\), LCM \(=1800\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
(i) HCF \(=3\), LCM \(=420\)
(ii) HCF \(=1\), LCM \(=11339\)
(iii) HCF \(=1\), LCM \(=1800\).
📋 Given

\(\mathrm{HCF}(306,657)=9\)

📘 Formula Used
\[\mathrm{HCF}\times\mathrm{LCM}=306\times657\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution
\[\mathrm{LCM}=\frac{306\times657}{9}\]
\[=\frac{306}{9}\times657\]
\[=34\times657\]
\[=22338\]
✅ Final Answer
\(\mathrm{LCM}(306,657)=22338\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
\(\mathrm{LCM}(306,657)=22338\).
📘 Key Idea

A number ending in \(0\) must be divisible by \(10=2\times5\).

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1: Prime factorise \(6^n\).

\[6^n=(2\times3)^n=2^n\times3^n\]

Step 2: Its prime factorisation contains only \(2\) and \(3\).

Step 3: There is no factor \(5\).

Step 4: Hence \(6^n\) is not divisible by \(10\), so it cannot end in \(0\).

✅ Final Answer
No. \(6^n\) cannot end with the digit \(0\) for any natural number \(n\).
🌐 Gujarati Translation
ના. કોઈપણ પ્રાકૃતિક સંખ્યા \(n\) માટે \(6^n\)નો અંત \(0\)થી થઈ શકતો નથી.
📘 Key Idea

A number is composite if it can be written as a product of two integers greater than \(1\).

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

(i) \(7\times11\times13+13\)

Take \(13\) as a common factor.

\[7\times11\times13+13=13(7\times11+1)\]
\[=13(77+1)=13\times78=1014\]

Both \(13\) and \(78\) are greater than \(1\), so \(1014\) is composite.

(ii) \(7\times6\times5\times4\times3\times2\times1+5\)

Take \(5\) as a common factor.

\[7\times6\times5\times4\times3\times2\times1+5\]
\[=5(7\times6\times4\times3\times2\times1+1)\]
\[=5(1008+1)=5\times1009=5045\]

Both \(5\) and \(1009\) are greater than \(1\), so \(5045\) is composite.

✅ Final Answer
\(7\times11\times13+13=13\times78\) and \(7\times6\times5\times4\times3\times2\times1+5=5\times1009\). Therefore, both numbers are composite.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
પ્રથમ સંખ્યા \(13\times78\) અને બીજી સંખ્યા \(5\times1009\) તરીકે લખાય છે; તેથી બંને સંયુક્ત સંખ્યાઓ છે.
📋 Given

Sonia completes one round in \(18\) minutes.

Ravi completes one round in \(12\) minutes.

🎯 To Find

The first time at which both complete a whole number of rounds together. This is \(\mathrm{LCM}(18,12)\).

✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

Step 1: Prime factorise.

\[18=2\times3^2\]
\[12=2^2\times3\]

Step 2: Take the greatest power of each prime.

\[\mathrm{LCM}(18,12)=2^2\times3^2\]
\[=4\times9=36\]

After \(36\) minutes, Sonia completes \(36/18=2\) rounds and Ravi completes \(36/12=3\) rounds.

✅ Final Answer
They will meet again at the starting point after \(36\) minutes.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
તેઓ \(36\) મિનિટ પછી ફરી શરૂઆતના બિંદુએ મળશે.

Exercise 1.2Revisiting Irrational Numbers

📘 Method Used

Proof by contradiction.

✏️ Step-by-Step Proof

Step 1: Assume that \(\sqrt5\) is rational.

Then, in lowest terms,

\[\sqrt5=\frac{a}{b}\]

where \(a,b\) are coprime integers and \(b\ne0\).

Step 2: Square both sides.

\[5=\frac{a^2}{b^2}\]
\[a^2=5b^2\]

Thus \(5\mid a^2\). Since \(5\) is prime, \(5\mid a\).

Step 3: Let \(a=5c\).

\[(5c)^2=5b^2\]
\[25c^2=5b^2\]
\[b^2=5c^2\]

Hence \(5\mid b^2\), so \(5\mid b\).

Step 4: Therefore \(5\) divides both \(a\) and \(b\), contradicting that they are coprime.

✅ Final Answer
\(\sqrt5\) is irrational.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
\(\sqrt5\) અપરિમેય સંખ્યા છે.
📘 Method Used

Proof by contradiction, using the known fact that \(\sqrt5\) is irrational.

✏️ Step-by-Step Proof

Step 1: Assume that \(3+2\sqrt5\) is rational.

Let

\[3+2\sqrt5=r\]

where \(r\) is rational.

Step 2: Isolate \(\sqrt5\).

\[2\sqrt5=r-3\]
\[\sqrt5=\frac{r-3}{2}\]

Step 3: Since \(r\), \(3\), and \(2\) are rational, \(\frac{r-3}{2}\) is rational.

This implies that \(\sqrt5\) is rational.

Step 4: But \(\sqrt5\) is irrational. This is a contradiction.

✅ Final Answer
\(3+2\sqrt5\) is irrational.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
\(3+2\sqrt5\) અપરિમેય સંખ્યા છે.
✏️ Step-by-Step Proof

(i) \(\dfrac{1}{\sqrt2}\)

Assume \(\dfrac1{\sqrt2}\) is rational and equals \(r\), where \(r\ne0\).

\[\frac1{\sqrt2}=r\]
\[\sqrt2=\frac1r\]

Since \(r\) is non-zero rational, \(\frac1r\) is rational. This would make \(\sqrt2\) rational, a contradiction.

(ii) \(7\sqrt5\)

Assume \(7\sqrt5\) is rational and equals \(r\).

\[7\sqrt5=r\]
\[\sqrt5=\frac r7\]

Because \(r/7\) is rational, this would make \(\sqrt5\) rational, a contradiction.

(iii) \(6+\sqrt2\)

Assume \(6+\sqrt2\) is rational and equals \(r\).

\[6+\sqrt2=r\]
\[\sqrt2=r-6\]

Since \(r-6\) is rational, this would make \(\sqrt2\) rational, a contradiction.

✅ Final Answer
\(\dfrac1{\sqrt2}\), \(7\sqrt5\), and \(6+\sqrt2\) are all irrational.
🌐 Gujarati Translation
\(\dfrac1{\sqrt2}\), \(7\sqrt5\) અને \(6+\sqrt2\) ત્રણેય અપરિમેય સંખ્યાઓ છે.
