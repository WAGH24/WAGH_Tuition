1.1 Physical Nature of MatterMatter is particulate and its particles are extremely small

1.2 Characteristics of Particles of MatterSpace, motion and force of attraction

In-text Question Set 1After Characteristics of Particles of Matter

✅ Answer

Chair, air, almonds, lemon water and the particles responsible for the smell of perfume are matter. Love, hate, thought and cold are not matter.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Matter has mass and occupies space.
  2. Chair, air, almonds and lemon water satisfy both conditions.
  3. Perfume vapour consists of material particles; love, hate and thought are feelings or ideas, while cold describes a condition.
✅ Final Answer

Chair, air, almonds, lemon water and the particles responsible for the smell of perfume are matter. Love, hate, thought and cold are not matter.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

ખુરશી, હવા, બદામ, લીંબુ પાણી અને પરફ્યુમની સુગંધ આપતા કણો દ્રવ્ય છે; પ્રેમ, દ્વેષ, વિચાર અને ઠંડી દ્રવ્ય નથી.

✅ Answer

Hot food gives its aroma particles more kinetic energy, so they diffuse through air faster and travel farther.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Particles of matter are continuously moving.
  2. Heating increases their kinetic energy.
  3. Therefore aroma particles from hot food spread faster than those from cold food.
✅ Final Answer

Hot food gives its aroma particles more kinetic energy, so they diffuse through air faster and travel farther.

✅ Answer

It shows that particles of matter have spaces between them and that the force of attraction in a liquid is not very strong.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Water particles are close but are not fixed in position.
  2. They can move aside when the diver applies force.
  3. They then flow back and occupy the space again.
✅ Final Answer

It shows that particles of matter have spaces between them and that the force of attraction in a liquid is not very strong.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

આ દર્શાવે છે કે પાણીના કણો વચ્ચે જગ્યા હોય છે અને દ્રવમાં કણોનું આકર્ષણ ઘન કરતાં ઓછું હોય છે.

✅ Answer

Particles of matter are extremely small, have spaces between them, are continuously moving and attract one another.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. They are very small—beyond ordinary visual observation.
  2. There are spaces between neighbouring particles.
  3. They possess kinetic energy and are always moving.
  4. A force of attraction acts between them.
✅ Final Answer

Particles of matter are extremely small, have spaces between them, are continuously moving and attract one another.

1.3 States of MatterSolid, liquid and gas

In-text Question Set 2After States of Matter

✅ Answer

The expected increasing order is: air < exhaust from chimneys < cotton < water < honey < chalk < iron.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Gases have the lowest densities among the listed materials.
  2. Cotton contains much trapped air, so its bulk density is low.
  3. Water and honey are liquids; honey is denser than water.
  4. Chalk and iron are solids; iron is the densest of the listed substances.
✅ Final Answer

The expected increasing order is: air < exhaust from chimneys < cotton < water < honey < chalk < iron.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

વધતી ઘનતાનો અપેક્ષિત ક્રમ: હવા < ચીમનીનો ધુમાડો < કપાસ < પાણી < મધ < ચોક < લોખંડ.

✅ Answer

The three states differ because their particles have different spacing, attraction and kinetic energy.

✏️ Key Interpretation
  1. Solids are rigid because their particles occupy fixed positions.
  2. Liquids flow because layers of particles can slide over one another.
  3. Gases are highly compressible and fill the entire container because their particles are far apart and move randomly at high speed.
✅ Final Answer

Solids, liquids and gases show the differences tabulated above.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

ઘન, દ્રવ અને વાયુમાં આકાર, ઘનફળ, સંકોચ્યતા, પ્રવાહિતા, ગતિજ ઊર્જા, ઘનતા અને કણોના આકર્ષણમાં ઉપર દર્શાવ્યા મુજબ તફાવત હોય છે.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation

(a)

Gas particles move freely in all directions and have negligible attraction, so they spread through all available space.

(b)

Rapidly moving gas particles repeatedly collide with the container walls. The force of these collisions per unit area produces pressure.

(c)

A wooden table has a definite shape and volume, is rigid and is nearly incompressible; therefore it is a solid.

(d)

Air particles are far apart and have weak attraction, so the hand passes through easily. Wood particles are tightly packed and strongly attracted, so a large force is required to separate them.

✅ Final Answer

The observations follow from particle spacing, motion and attraction in gases and solids.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

આ અવલોકનો વાયુ અને ઘનમાં કણોની જગ્યા, ગતિ અને આકર્ષણના તફાવતને કારણે થાય છે.

✅ Answer

Ice floats because its density is lower than the density of liquid water.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. When water freezes, hydrogen bonding produces an open crystal arrangement.
  2. The same mass occupies a larger volume in ice.
  3. Since density equals mass divided by volume, the density decreases.
  4. A less-dense substance floats on a denser liquid.
✅ Final Answer

Ice floats because its density is lower than the density of liquid water.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

બરફની રચના ખુલ્લી હોવાથી સમાન દ્રવ્યમાન વધુ ઘનફળ લે છે; તેથી બરફની ઘનતા પાણી કરતાં ઓછી હોય છે અને તે તરશે.

1.4 Can Matter Change its State?Effect of temperature and pressure

In-text Question Set 3After Change of State

📘 Formula Used
\[T(^{\circ}\mathrm C)=T(\mathrm K)-273\]
✏️ Step-by-Step Solution

(a) 300 K

\[300-273=27^{\circ}\mathrm C\]

(b) 573 K

\[573-273=300^{\circ}\mathrm C\]
✅ Final Answer

(a) 27°C    (b) 300°C

🌐 Gujarati Translation

(a) 27°C    (b) 300°C

✅ Answer

(a) At 250°C, water is in the gaseous state (steam). (b) At 100°C and normal atmospheric pressure, liquid water and water vapour can coexist during boiling.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. The normal boiling point of water is 100°C.
  2. A temperature of 250°C is well above the boiling point, so water is vapour.
  3. At exactly 100°C during boiling, liquid changes into vapour, so both states may be present.
✅ Final Answer

(a) At 250°C, water is in the gaseous state (steam). (b) At 100°C and normal atmospheric pressure, liquid water and water vapour can coexist during boiling.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

(a) 250°C પર પાણી વાયુ અવસ્થામાં છે. (b) 100°C પર ઉકળતી વખતે દ્રવ પાણી અને જળવાષ્પ બંને રહી શકે છે.

✅ Answer

The temperature remains constant because the supplied heat is absorbed as latent heat to overcome the force of attraction between particles.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Temperature measures the average kinetic energy of particles.
  2. During melting or boiling, the added energy increases particle separation instead of kinetic energy.
  3. Therefore the temperature does not rise until the phase change is complete.
✅ Final Answer

The temperature remains constant because the supplied heat is absorbed as latent heat to overcome the force of attraction between particles.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

અવસ્થાપરિવર્તન દરમિયાન ગરમી કણો વચ્ચેનું આકર્ષણ દૂર કરવા ગુપ્ત ઉષ્મા તરીકે વપરાય છે, તેથી પરિવર્તન પૂર્ણ થાય ત્યાં સુધી તાપમાન સ્થિર રહે છે.

✅ Answer

Atmospheric gases can be liquefied by applying high pressure and lowering the temperature.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Cooling reduces the kinetic energy of gas particles.
  2. Compression brings the particles closer together.
  3. At sufficiently low temperature and high pressure, attractive forces convert the gas into a liquid.
✅ Final Answer

Atmospheric gases can be liquefied by applying high pressure and lowering the temperature.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

વાયુને ઠંડો કરીને અને તેના પર ઊંચું દબાણ લગાવી વાયુમંડલીય વાયુઓનું દ્રવીકરણ કરી શકાય છે.

1.5 EvaporationFactors affecting evaporation and cooling

In-text Question Set 4After Evaporation

✅ Answer

On a hot, dry day the temperature is high and humidity is low, so water evaporates rapidly and absorbs more heat from the surrounding air.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. High temperature supplies energy for evaporation.
  2. Low humidity allows air to accept more water vapour.
  3. Rapid evaporation removes latent heat and produces greater cooling.
✅ Final Answer

On a hot, dry day the temperature is high and humidity is low, so water evaporates rapidly and absorbs more heat from the surrounding air.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

ગરમ અને સૂકા દિવસે તાપમાન વધારે તથા ભેજ ઓછી હોવાથી પાણી ઝડપથી બાષ્પીભવન કરે છે અને વધુ ઠંડક આપે છે.

✅ Answer

A small amount of water seeps through the tiny pores of the pot and evaporates from the outer surface, taking heat from the remaining water and the pot.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. An earthen pot has microscopic pores.
  2. Water reaching the outer surface evaporates.
  3. The latent heat required is taken from the pot and its contents, lowering their temperature.
✅ Final Answer

A small amount of water seeps through the tiny pores of the pot and evaporates from the outer surface, taking heat from the remaining water and the pot.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

માટલાના સૂક્ષ્મ છિદ્રોથી બહાર આવેલું પાણી બાષ્પીભવન માટે ગરમી શોષે છે, તેથી અંદરનું પાણી ઠંડું થાય છે.

✅ Answer

These volatile liquids evaporate rapidly and absorb latent heat from the palm, so the palm feels cold.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Their particles escape easily into the vapour state.
  2. Evaporation requires energy.
  3. That energy is drawn from the skin, reducing its temperature.
✅ Final Answer

These volatile liquids evaporate rapidly and absorb latent heat from the palm, so the palm feels cold.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

એસિટોન, પેટ્રોલ અથવા પરફ્યુમ ઝડપથી બાષ્પીભવન કરે છે અને હથેળીમાંથી ગરમી શોષે છે, તેથી હથેળી ઠંડી લાગે છે.

✅ Answer

A saucer exposes a larger surface area, increasing evaporation and cooling the liquid faster.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. The same liquid spreads into a thin layer in a saucer.
  2. Its exposed surface area becomes larger.
  3. Faster evaporation removes heat more quickly, so it can be sipped sooner.
✅ Final Answer

A saucer exposes a larger surface area, increasing evaporation and cooling the liquid faster.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

રકાબીમાં સપાટી વિસ્તાર વધારે હોવાથી બાષ્પીભવન અને ઠંડક ઝડપથી થાય છે; તેથી ગરમ ચા અથવા દૂધ વહેલું પી શકાય છે.

✅ Answer

We should wear light-coloured, loose cotton clothes in summer.

✏️ Step-by-Step Explanation
  1. Cotton absorbs sweat well.
  2. It spreads sweat over a larger area and permits easy evaporation.
  3. Evaporation removes heat from the body; light colours absorb less solar heat and loose clothes improve air circulation.
✅ Final Answer

We should wear light-coloured, loose cotton clothes in summer.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

ઉનાળામાં હળવા રંગનાં ઢીલાં સુતરાઉ કપડાં પહેરવા જોઈએ, કારણ કે તે પરસેવો શોષી તેના બાષ્પીભવનથી શરીરને ઠંડું રાખે છે.

  4. Therefore it removes more heat and provides stronger cooling.
✅ Final Answer

Ice at 273 K absorbs additional latent heat of fusion while melting, whereas water at 273 K does not need to absorb this extra heat.

🌐 Gujarati Translation

273 K પર બરફ પીગળતાં વધારાની ગુપ્ત ગલન ઉષ્મા શોષે છે; તેથી સમાન તાપમાનના પાણી કરતાં વધુ ઠંડક આપે છે.

  1. Steam contains the heat corresponding to 100°C plus latent heat of vaporisation.

  3. During condensation it releases this latent heat to the skin, transferring more energy.
✅ Final Answer

Steam produces more severe burns than boiling water at the same temperature.
