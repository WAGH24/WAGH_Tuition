In-text Question Set 212.1.1 Crop Variety Improvement

✅ Answer

Biotic and abiotic stresses reduce crop growth, yield and quality. Biotic factors include diseases, insects and nematodes; abiotic factors include drought, salinity, waterlogging, heat, cold and frost.

✏️ Detailed Explanation
  1. Biotic factors are living agents. They damage leaves, stems, roots, flowers, grains or stored produce and may spread disease.
  2. Abiotic factors are non-living environmental stresses. They disturb water uptake, photosynthesis, respiration and other physiological processes.
  3. Both types of stress can cause weak growth, poor flowering, fewer grains or fruits, and sometimes complete crop failure.
  4. Growing resistant and widely adaptable crop varieties reduces these losses.
✅ Final Answer

Biotic agents and adverse environmental conditions lower crop yield and quality; resistant varieties help minimise the damage.

Tallness and profuse branching are desirable in fodder crops, while dwarfness is desirable in cereals.

✏️ Detailed Explanation
  1. Fodder crops are grown mainly for vegetative biomass.
  2. Therefore tall plants with many branches provide more leaves and stems for livestock feed.
  3. Cereal crops are grown mainly for grains.
  4. Dwarf cereal plants use fewer nutrients for excessive stem growth, resist lodging and direct more resources towards grain production.
✅ Final Answer

Fodder crops should be tall and highly branched, whereas cereal crops should preferably be dwarf.

In-text Question Set 612.1.3 Crop Protection Management

✅ Answer

They should be preferred because they control pests with less harm to crops, beneficial organisms, soil, water, animals and human health than excessive chemical pesticides.

✏️ Detailed Explanation
  1. Preventive measures—clean seed beds, timely sowing, crop rotation, intercropping, resistant varieties and summer ploughing—stop pests from building up.
  2. Biological control uses natural enemies or bio-agents against pests and diseases.
  3. These methods leave little or no harmful chemical residue on food.
  4. They protect pollinators, predators and other useful organisms.
  5. They reduce environmental pollution and slow the development of pesticide resistance.
✅ Final Answer

Preventive and biological methods are safer, eco-friendly and sustainable, and they protect non-target organisms while controlling crop pests.

In-text Question Set 712.2.1 Cattle Farming

✅ Answer

Cross-breeding is commonly used. Exotic breeds with long lactation and high milk production are crossed with indigenous breeds that have good disease resistance and adaptation to local climate.

✏️ Detailed Explanation
  1. Select an exotic breed such as Jersey or Brown Swiss for long lactation and high milk yield.
  2. Select an indigenous breed such as Red Sindhi or Sahiwal for disease resistance and local adaptability.
  3. Cross the two breeds.
  4. Select offspring that combine the desirable qualities of both parents.
✅ Final Answer

Cross-breeding combines the high milk yield and long lactation of exotic cattle with the disease resistance and adaptability of indigenous cattle.

Poultry birds can eat inexpensive agricultural by-products and other feed materials that humans do not normally consume and efficiently convert them into eggs and meat, both rich sources of animal protein.

  1. Many agricultural by-products have little direct food value for humans.

Poultry efficiently turns low-value, non-human food materials into protein-rich eggs and meat, reducing waste and producing affordable animal protein.

In-text Question Set 1012.2.3 Fish Production

✅ Answer

Fish are obtained in two main ways: capture fishing from natural water resources and culture fishery, in which fish are reared through fish farming.

✏️ Detailed Explanation
  1. Capture fishing: naturally occurring fish are caught from seas, rivers, lakes, reservoirs and other water bodies.
  2. Culture fishery: selected fish or shellfish are bred and reared under managed conditions.
  3. Both capture and culture can occur in marine water and inland fresh or brackish water.
✅ Final Answer

Fish are obtained either by capture fishing from natural resources or by culture fishery through fish farming.

In-text Question Set 1112.2.4 Bee-keeping

✅ Answer

Desirable bee varieties should collect large quantities of honey, sting less, remain in the hive for long periods and breed well.

✏️ Detailed Explanation
  1. High honey-collection capacity increases yield.
  2. Less aggressive bees are easier and safer to manage.
  3. Bees that remain in a hive for long periods reduce colony loss or swarming.
  4. Good breeding ability maintains a strong worker population.
  5. The Italian bee, Apis mellifera, possesses many of these traits and is widely used commercially.
✅ Final Answer

High honey yield, less stinging, long hive stay and good breeding capacity are desirable traits.

PART II — Chapter End Exercises

✅ Answer

Intercropping is one method that can ensure high and stable yield. Two or more crops are grown simultaneously on the same field in a definite row pattern, such as soybean and maize.

✏️ Detailed Explanation
  1. Choose crops with different nutrient needs, root depths or growth habits.
  2. Sow them in a fixed row pattern, for example one row of one crop followed by one or two rows of another.
  3. The crops use sunlight, water, space and soil nutrients more efficiently.
  4. The spread of pests and diseases is often reduced because the field does not contain a single continuous host crop.
  5. If one crop performs poorly, the other may still yield well, reducing risk.
  6. The produce of each crop can be harvested separately because rows are distinct.
✅ Final Answer

Intercropping improves resource use, lowers pest and disease spread, reduces risk and can increase the total yield from a field.

✅ Answer

They are used to replenish essential plant nutrients removed from the soil by repeated cropping and thereby improve crop growth and yield.

✏️ Detailed Explanation
  1. Crops absorb mineral nutrients from soil throughout their growth.
  2. Harvesting removes many of these nutrients from the field.
  3. Manure returns organic matter and small amounts of nutrients, improves soil structure and supports soil organisms.
  4. Fertilizers provide specific nutrients such as nitrogen, phosphorus and potassium rapidly.
  5. Judicious combined use restores fertility and supports healthy, high-yielding crops.
✅ Final Answer

Manure and fertilizers replace soil nutrients, improve fertility and support better crop growth and yield.

✅ Answer

Genetic manipulation is the deliberate alteration or transfer of genes to introduce desired characteristics into an organism. In agriculture it is used to develop crop varieties with useful traits.

✏️ Detailed Explanation
  1. Identify a desirable character such as disease resistance, stress tolerance or improved quality.
  2. Locate or select the gene responsible for that character.
  3. Introduce the gene into a crop variety or combine it through an appropriate breeding programme.
  4. Select plants that express the desired trait and test them under field conditions.
  5. Multiply suitable plants to produce an improved variety.

Useful outcomes include higher yield, better quality, resistance to insects and diseases, tolerance to drought or salinity, shorter maturity duration and wider adaptability.

✅ Final Answer

Genetic manipulation changes or introduces genes to produce improved crop varieties with higher yield, better quality and resistance to biotic or abiotic stresses.

✅ Answer

Storage losses occur when grains are attacked by insects, rodents, mites, fungi or bacteria, or when they are stored under unsuitable moisture and temperature conditions.

✏️ Process of Loss
  1. Grains stored with excess moisture favour fungal and bacterial growth.
  2. Warm or poorly ventilated storage may increase insect and mite multiplication.
  3. Rodents and insects eat grains and contaminate them with waste.
  4. Damage reduces grain weight, colour, quality and germination capacity.
  5. The produce then has poor marketability and may become unsafe.

Strict cleaning, proper sun- and shade-drying, dry storage, inspection and suitable fumigation reduce losses.

✅ Final Answer

Biotic agents and improper storage moisture or temperature degrade grains, reduce weight and germination, and cause discolouration and spoilage.
